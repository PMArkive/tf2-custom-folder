	Some information:
		The $Origin value controls where the weapon/animation gets put on the screen.
		$Orgin 30 30 0 = $Origin <x> <y> <-z> 
		More helpful information about this here: https://developer.valvesoftware.com/wiki/$origin
		For example, I use $Origin 0 20 15 to get some very small viewmodels for a weapon/animation.
		To sum it up a bit:
			A positive X value will move the weapon closer to the center.
			A negative X value will move the weapon away from the center, based on which hand holds it.
			A positive Y value will move the weapon away from the camera, towards the crosshair.
			A negative Y value will move the weapon closer to the camera, away from the crosshair.
			A positive Z value will move the weapon down, towards the bottom of the screen.
			A negative Z value will move the weapon up, towards the top of the screen.
			Theres the optional Z rotation value you can add after X Y Z, it will rotate the weapon.
		
		For (Y/N) options, just change [N] to [Y] if you want to use them.
		â˜›KEEP IN MIND it's case sensitive, it has to be [Y], not [y].
